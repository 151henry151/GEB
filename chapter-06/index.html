Chapter VI: The Location of Meaning

Meaning lives in the message, the decoder, and the receiver together. The same marks can mean different things with a different decoder.

Message, decoder, receiver

Meaning is split among the message, the decoder, and the receiver. Change the decoder and the meaning of the message can change.

The location of meaning

Hofstadter discusses how meaning is distributed among message, decoder, and context. Symbols in a formal system have no intrinsic meaning until we provide an interpretation.
