Chapter IX: Mumon and Gödel

A sentence says: You cannot prove this sentence with the rules. If you could prove it, the game would break. So the game cannot prove it. But then the sentence is true. So something is true even though the game cannot prove it.

Gödel's trick

Gödel built a sentence of TNT that says I am not a theorem of TNT. If TNT could prove it, TNT would be inconsistent. So if TNT is consistent, it cannot prove that sentence. So the sentence is true but unprovable.

Mumon and Zen

The chapter links this to Zen and mu. The paradox is avoided by distinguishing proof-in-the-system from truth.

Self-reference and incompleteness

Hofstadter presents Gödel's construction: a formula G that expresses I am not provable in TNT. If TNT is consistent, G is not provable; and G is true. So truth and provability come apart. The chapter connects this to the liar paradox and to Zen (Mumon).
