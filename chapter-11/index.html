Chapter XI: Brains and Thoughts

Your brain is made of tiny cells that turn on and off and pass signals. Thoughts might be like patterns of those signals. So thinking could be something that happens when lots of little parts follow rules together.

Brains as physical systems

Neurons, symbols, and levels of description. Can mental states be understood as physical states? The chapter explores how brain activity might realize thought.

Reductionism and holism

The Ant Fugue raises the question: is the colony conscious or only the ants? Levels of description apply to brains too.

Brains and thoughts

Hofstadter discusses the relationship between neural activity and symbolic thought. Brains can be described at multiple levels; the question of whether thought is reducible to physics or emerges at a higher level is framed in terms of the book themes.
