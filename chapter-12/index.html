Chapter XII: Minds and Thoughts

The same mark or sound can mean different things depending on where it is. An arrow up might mean "go up" in one game and "the number five" in another. So "meaning" depends on context — who is reading it and what game they're in.

Minds and intentionality

Mind, meaning, and "aboutness." Can minds be understood as multi-level systems? The location of meaning (message, decoder, receiver) applies to mental content too.

Symbol grounding

Symbols in a system get meaning from how they're used and what they're connected to. The same symbol in two contexts can have two meanings.

Minds and thoughts

Hofstadter explores whether minds can be understood in the same multilevel, symbolic framework as computers and brains. Intentionality and the "grounding" of symbols in context are discussed in relation to the book's themes.
